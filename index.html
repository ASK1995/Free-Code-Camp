Sachin Tendulkar

The man with 100 centuries
Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar was considered to be the best batsman the world has ever seen, said to be reminiscient of Sir Donald Bradman during his years of play with records unmatchable by anyone else in the sport.

    Time line of Sachin's life:

  • 1989 - Test Debut vs Pakistan
  • 1994 - Opened for 1st time vs New Zealand
  • 1996 - Captain of Indian Team
  • 2000 - Quit Captaincy
  • 2002 - Equals Bradman's record of 29 Test Centuries
  • 2003 - Highest Run Scorer and Man of the series award in World cup
  • 2008 - Surpassed Brian Lara to become top run scorer in tests
  • 2011 - Won 1st world cup
  • 2012 - 100th century
  • 2013 - IPL team Mumbai Indians Captain
  • 2015 - Mumbai Indians win IPL
  • 2014 - Retires from Test Cricket
  • 2015 - Wins IPL Trophy again and retires from IPL

"Sachin has been given the Nickname of Little Master or Master Blaster during his sandstorm innings of 143 vs the aussies at Sharjah!"

-- Harsha Bhogle
